Exterior Car Detailing That Delivers

It’s more than soap and water. When we talk about exterior car detailing, we’re talking about a full top-to-bottom treatment of your vehicle’s outer surfaces. The goal? A deep clean that makes your car shine like new, and helps protect it for the road ahead.

Here’s how we do it:

  • Gentle Hand Wash & Dry – No spinning brushes or harsh chemicals. Just expert hands, quality mitts, and soft microfiber towels that get the job done safely.
  • Clay Bar Treatment – This step pulls out stubborn debris from your paint surface, stuff that’s been stuck there for months.
  • Paint Decontamination – Iron particles and other road grime don’t stand a chance.
  • Polishing or Paint Correction – If your car’s paint looks cloudy or has light swirl marks, we buff it back to life.
  • Wax or Sealant Application – For that mirror-like finish and long-lasting protection from UV rays, dust, and whatever the Albuquerque weather throws your way.

We also clean your wheels and dress your tires, so the whole car looks cohesive, not just the paint.

That’s where we come in.

In short: it’s not just a detail, it’s part of your vehicle’s maintenance routine.

How Often Should You Detail Your Car’s Exterior?

We get this question a lot, and honestly, it depends.

If your car sits outside all day, gets driven frequently, or is exposed to a lot of dust, bugs, or bird droppings (hello, New Mexico pigeons), then every 3 to 4 months is a great rhythm. That keeps your paint in good shape and your vehicle always looking fresh.

But if you only use your car occasionally or park in a garage most of the time, every 6 months might do the trick.

Detailing frequency isn’t one-size-fits-all, it depends on how and where you drive. Consistency, though, is key to keeping your exterior in its best shape long-term.

Not sure what’s best? No problem. Just give us a call or stop by, we’re happy to take a look and offer honest advice.

Protection That Stays With You

One of the biggest benefits of regular detailing is protection. We don’t just make your car look good, we help keep it that way.

When we apply wax or sealant, we’re putting a barrier between your paint and the outside world. That layer helps shield your finish from sun damage, bird droppings, tree sap, and road grime. And that means fewer scratches, fewer stains, and a paint job that holds its color longer.

A Few Tips Between Visits

While we’re always happy to see you, here are a few simple things you can do to keep your car looking sharp between details:

  • Rinse regularly. Dust and dirt can scratch your paint if left too long.
  • Avoid wiping with dry towels. Always use a soft, damp microfiber cloth.
  • Park in shade when possible. Sun exposure wears down your clear coat.
  • Get to bird droppings quickly. They can damage paint if left too long.
